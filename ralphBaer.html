Who is Ralph Baer?

Ralph Baer was born on March 8th, 1922 in Pirmasens, Germany. He died on December 6th, 2014 in Manchester, New Hampshire. He was an American engineer and inventor that is known as the father of video games for his role in developing the earliest home video-game console.

About Ralph's life

Baer;s family fled Nazi Germany and moved to New York, where he took an interest in technology. He work for the military as an intelligence officer for 3 years. When he got back he started going to the American Television Institute of Technology in Chicago and graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Television Engineering in 1949. In 1996 he came up with the idea of how he could build games into televisions. After Baer licensed the console Magnavox, which released the device as the Magnavox Odyssey in 1972.

What is the Odyssey?

The Magnavox Odyssey was the World's first home video game console. The Magnavox Odyssey was a cartridge based console allowing it to play a different game simply by inserting a different game card. Although the console itself was black and white, colour was added to the games by use of a screen overlay that attached to the screen of your CRT television.
